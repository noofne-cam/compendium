> **Field Theorem.** There exists a unique field with a total order satisfying the supremum condition. This field is called $\mathbf R$, the field of **real numbers**. Furthermore, there exists a natural isomorphism between $\mathbf Q$ and a subset of $\mathbf R$, which we identify automatically with the rational field.
---
- **I.** *Dedekind Construction of $\mathbf R$*.
	- ; Essentially, we associate the elements of $\mathbf R$ with certain subsets of $\mathbf Q$, called *Dedekind cuts*, and we may think of the *value* of a given $\Omega \subset \mathbf Q$ as being $\sup \Omega$, even if it may not exist in $\mathbf Q$. From this the field operations are defined naturally, and it is shown that this construction does indeed satisfy the properties of the real field.
